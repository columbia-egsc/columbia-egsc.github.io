Please describe any relevant past experience

During my undergraduate degree in the UK I was for two consecutive years (2011-2013) a member of the Cambridge University Cypriot Society, as an IT and then as a Vice-President of the committee. In my position as an IT representative I was serving the role of providing the means of communication between the committee and the members of the society, while I also promoting the society through the internet by contacting new students and bringing them in direct contact with the rest of the society. As a Vice-President I was responsible for planning a number of events ranging from socials with board games to nights out in clubs, which involved the responsibilities of creating the schedule of events, booking deals with venues, managing budgets and collecting useful feedback from the members for future improvements. Between 2013-2014 I was also a representative for my year of the Staff-Student Committee member of the Chemical Engineering Cambridge Department. As a member of this committee I had the opportunity to formally express the ideas and complains of my classmates about the department and the courses and try to work out alternatives together with the Professors involved in the committee about the different issues.

What are your goals/vision for your committees of choice? How do you think you can contribute to them?

As a member of the Alumni Affairs committee I will get the chance to contribute towards the organisation of a number of events based on students' requests and preferences that will bring students in direct contact with Alumni. Such events can vary from Alumni talks that followed an engineering-based career path after their graduate program in Columbia to events based on Alumni that followed a number of non-engineering related career paths after Columbia. In this way we will be able to meet successful people in their careers and get ideas about the different alternatives that one can seek in the job market. Alumni affairs committee can also work closer with the Center for Career Education to contact Alumni and provide the opportunity for graduate engineers to have a more direct access to Alumni through EGSC.
