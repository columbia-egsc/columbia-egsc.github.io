As part of the School's 150th anniversary celebration, Dean Boyce has announced a video contest for students with a top prize of $2,000 for the winning video.

 Topic: The video should capture the significant impacts of engineering in shaping our world over the past 100 or so years and its promise and envisioned impacts to continue to revolutionize our world and our lives over the coming decades. It would be great to focus on Columbia Engineering but that is not a requirement. 

Video format: The format is totally open – you can include photos, sketches, animations, movies, words, music, voice overs, people, processes, artifacts, things...use your imagination! Videos may be done by individuals or groups, undergrads or grads or combinations of both!

Length: Between 1½ and 2½ minutes

Deadline: Monday, November 3, at 12 noon

Judging: ESC and EGSC leadership will consider the entire field of videos and select 5 top entries. Those entries will be submitted to the dean and a faculty panel for review, and following that process, the final winner will be selected by the students via online voting.

Prize: $2,000 for the winning undergraduate video and $2000 for the winning graduate video. The winning videos will be shown on Friday, November 14 during the 150th anniversary symposium and will be featured on the School's home page and 150th website.

Eligibility: All undergraduate and graduate students in SEAS (teams may have one member from another University school).

Model videos: The National Academy of Engineering held a similar contest. Here is a link to their winning videos, but please use your own imagination for your video!

Submission: Teams having a majority of graduate students should submit their videos to videos@egsc.seas.columbia.edu. The videos should be of atleast 720p resolution. Any questions can be directed to nikul@egsc.seas.columbia.edu

EGSC Professional Development Scholarship

In keeping with the highest ideals of the EGSC, the Professional Development Scholarship Program is awarded to outstanding graduate students in The Columbia University Fu Foundation School of Engineering and Applied Sciences (SEAS) as a means of defraying costs associated with their academic and professional endeavors. In addition to a monetary award, EGSC Professional Development Scholars are provided a platform from which to disseminate their knowledge and expertise, thereby impacting the larger SEAS community.

This award carries no citizenship requirements. Eligible recipients must be current graduate students (M.S., Ph.D., or M.S. leading to Ph.D.) enrolled full-time and in good academic standing. Special consideration may be given to applicants who demonstrate financial need and/or show evidence of self-support. Current members of the EGSC Executive Board and the University Senator are NOT eligible to receive funding during their tenure.

Applicants are limited to one application per academic year, and resubmission of identical applications is discouraged. Awardees are not eligible to re-apply in subsequent years. Students found to be requesting reimbursement in duplicate to other funding programs will be stripped of the Scholar title, removed from consideration, and no longer eligible for funding in subsequent years.

Applicants may request up to $1000 per application.

Unused funds are non-transferrable and may not be used for expenses unrelated to those explicitly stated in the application. Please note that original itemized receipts and proof of acceptance/attendance (if applicable) are required in order to be eligible for reimbursement.

Please visit http://columbia-egsc.github.io/scholarship.html for more details and the application form

President Bollinger's Fireside Chat

President Bollinger holds Fireside Chats during each term to give students an informal venue to engage with him and ask questions about the University, administration, student life, and other topics. This semester, President Bollinger will be holding the Fireside Chat for graduate students on November 10th, 2014 from 6:00PM – 7:30PM at the President’s House, 60 Morningside Drive at 116th Street.

President Bollinger is inviting each graduate school to send a number of students based on the school’s proportion of overall graduate student enrollment.
Eleven (11) SEAS graduate students will have the opportunity to attend.  Given the limited number of slots for this event, students will be selected through a lottery system to ensure fairness.  If you would like to be considered for this event, please register your information here: https://www.surveymonkey.com/s/FiresideFall2014.  Students selected from this lottery will be asked to submit questions in advance to be sent to President Bollinger.
 
The lottery will take place on Friday, October 24, 2014.  Registration for the lottery will close Thursday, October 23, 2014 at midnight.
