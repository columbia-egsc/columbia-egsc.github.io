EGSC Professional Development Scholarships


PROGRAM DESCRIPTION:

In keeping with the highest ideals of the EGSC, the Professional Development Scholarship Program is awarded to outstanding graduate students in The Columbia University Fu Foundation School of Engineering and Applied Sciences (SEAS) as a means of defraying costs associated with their academic and professional endeavors. In addition to a monetary award, EGSC Professional Development Scholars are provided a platform from which to disseminate their knowledge and expertise, thereby impacting the larger SEAS community.

ELIGIBILITY:

This award carries no citizenship requirements. Eligible recipients must be current graduate students (M.S., Ph.D., or M.S. leading to Ph.D.) enrolled full-time and in good academic standing. Special consideration may be given to applicants who demonstrate financial need and/or show evidence of self-support. Current members of the EGSC Executive Board and the University Senator are NOT eligible to receive funding during their tenure.

Applicants are limited to one application per academic year, and resubmission of identical applications is discouraged. Awardees are not eligible to re-apply in subsequent years. Students found to be requesting reimbursement in duplicate to other funding programs will be stripped of the Scholar title, removed from consideration, and no longer eligible for funding in subsequent years.

ANTICIPATED AWARD AMOUNT:

Applicants may request up to $1000 per application.

Unused funds are non-transferrable and may not be used for expenses unrelated to those explicitly stated in the application. Please note that original itemized receipts and proof of acceptance/attendance (if applicable) are required in order to be eligible for reimbursement.

APPLICATION:


Find the application form here.

Completed applications must include: (1) EGSC Scholarship Application Form, (2) Student Scholar Statement, and (3) Curriculum Vitae. For conference or summer school travel, applicants should additionally include confirmation of acceptance into the program.

The Student Scholar Statement should address all of the following points:
  the intended use of the scholarship award (i.e. the specific fees to be reimbursed)
 the intellectual merit of the knowledge or experience the applicant will gain on a personal level via the scholarship
 the manner in which the acquired knowledge or experience will be disseminated by the applicant to the greater SEAS community
 the intellectual merit and broader impact of the knowledge or experience to be relayed to the greater SEAS community

The statement should be written on standard 8.5" x 11" page size, with 12-point Times New Roman font, 1" margins, and single line spacing. The statement should not exceed two pages.

Please note: reimbursement of expenses detailed in the application will only be provided upon successful dissemination of knowledge to the SEAS community.

APPLICATION DEADLINE: May 1st, 2014

For more details, visit http://columbia-egsc.github.io/scholarship.html

Pre-NASES Speaker Series 2 - Expert Outlook


Date: Wednesday, April 16th, 2014, 5pm-7pm

Location: Satow Room (5th Floor), Lerner Hall, Columbia University

Experts:

William Hewitt - Adjunct Faculty at NYU - Centre for Global Affairs and Author of "A Newer World"

Michael Gerrard - Professor at Columbia Law School and Director of the Centre for Climate Change Law, Earth Institute

Speaker Agenda

5:00 – 5:05 pm | Introduction to the event and first expert

5:05 – 5:30 pm | Interview - The role of media in the energy space

5:30 – 5:45 pm | Audience Q&A

5:45 – 5:50 pm | Introduction to second expert

5:50 – 6:15 pm | Interview - The ConEd rate case and a historic win

6:15 – 6:30 pm | Audience Q&A

6:30 | Thank you note

Session Description

Expert Interview 1: The Role of Media in the Energy Space

William "Bill" Hewitt has been an environmental professional and activist for the last 30 years. He is a writer and editor, as well as the principal of Hewitt Communications. His book "A Newer World – Politics, Money, Technology, and What’s Really Being Done to Solve the Climate Crisis" has provided a compelling rationale to take cogent actions towards solving the problem.

Janice, Co-founder of Student Energy will be taking Mr. Hewitt through the process of writing his book and all that has shaped his perspective these last many years and discussing just about how important a role media has in shaping action.

Expert Interview 2: The ConEd Rate Case and CCCL's Historic Win

Michael Gerrard is an environment, energy and climate law expert with experience of over 30 years. His illustrious career has involved trying numerous cases and arguing many appeals in federal and state courts and administrative tribunals, handling the environmental aspects of numerous transactions and development projects, and providing regulatory compliance advice to a wide variety of clients in the private and public sectors. Presently, he is using his abundant knowledge to educate students at CLS.

David Lyons, Co-President of the Environmental Law Society at Columbia will, through the chat with Mr. Gerrard, detail the historic legislation achieved by CCCL's efforts in ConEd's rate case to include climate change adaptation in their infrastructure development.

Summer Data Science Bootcamp


The Data Incubator is an intensive six-week bootcamp that prepares postdocs and PhD students in STEM + social science fields seeking industry careers as data scientists.  It identifies fellows who already have the 90% difficult-to-learn skills and equips them with the last 10%: the tools and technology stack that make them self-sufficient, strong, productive contributors.  The program is free and housing support is available.

The program will be from June 9 - July 18 in New York City.  Applications due April 16th.
