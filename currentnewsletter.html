iMentor Info Session



Looking for a high-impact, flexible volunteering opportunity? Send a weekly email and meet once a month to empower a student to graduate high school and pursue their college aspirations.

You can make a positive difference in the life of a high school student by volunteering with iMentor, which empowers students in low-income communities to graduate high school, succeed in college, and achieve their ambitions. iMentor pairs students with college-educated mentors in matches that last three to four years, to ensure students have consistent support during critical academic years. iMentor's curriculum and staff guide mentors as they work one-on-one with their mentees on four key actions: developing strong personal relationships, nurturing a college aspiration, navigating the college application process, and building critical skills linked to high school and college success. Mentors and mentees exchange one weekly email on a topic iMentor provides and meet once a month at group events iMentor organizes. Mentors are supported every step of the way with training sessions, networking events, and coaching from iMentor staff. The program offers enough flexibility for busy professionals to participate while ensuring students get the structure and guidance they need to reach their goals. Sign up to become a mentor today at www.imentor.org/become-a-mentor<http://www.imentor.org/become-a-mentor> ?



Interested in Learning More? Join us for an iMentor information session

September 10
3:00pm
Davis Auditorium


We will provide an overview of our school-based program, talk about how iMentor plays a role in the educational landscape and why college success is so important for NYC youth.

The guidance of a mentor can empower a young person to graduate high school college-ready, succeed in college and fulfill their potential. And it will be one of the most rewarding experiences of your life!

Mentors are:

College graduates, 21 years old and above
Professionals and graduates who live or work in the NYC metro area

Individuals who can commit six hours a month, on average, to their mentee - half in person and half online

Mentees are:

NYC public high school students
71% will be a first generation college student
80% live below the poverty line

As part of the iMentor community you will:
Join your mentee at an evening iMentor group event once a month for two hours
Read and respond to one email a week from your mentee
Develop a supportive mentoring relationship that will last from 3 - 4 years
Guide your mentee through iMentor's college-readiness curriculum

Abstract: Google Cardboard is an inexpensive, DIY virtual reality headset and open-source SDK that make it easy for anyone to experience and develop immersive virtual reality applications. The Columbia University Libraries/Information Services (CUL/IS) is conducting a study to explore use of the Libraries’ scholarly e-book collections for research, teaching, and learning purposes. We are inviting graduate and professional degree students to participate in focus groups to discuss e-book usage, preferences, and needs. If you are interested in participating in a focus group session, please complete this brief screening survey, http://bit.ly/ebookstudy, by Sunday, September 14, 2014. If you are selected to participate in the study, you will receive a date and time based on the availability you have indicated.
