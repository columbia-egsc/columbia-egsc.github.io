LEED Accreditation

Become LEED Accredited!

When: February 14th 2015 - 12:00 PM to 5:00 PM
Where: CCNY - Mechanical Engineering Building – Exhibition Room - http://goo.gl/maps/oJ3lG
Registration: http://leadinggreen.ca/NYC
Interested in getting involved in the Green Building Industry? Opportunities are plentiful in the field of sustainable design and LEED is at its forefront.
 
LEED (Leadership in Energy and Environmental Design) is simply a green-rating point system, or a scorecard. The more energy efficient and sustainable a building is, the more points it will earn. To date, this course and its materials have proven to be instrumental in helping over 1500 students pass their respective exam at a 100% pass rate. This course is offered at a quarter of the price and time as the competition and is geared at allowing students to graduate with letters after their name!
 
Just as Buildings can be LEED certified, people in the sustainable construction industry can become LEED Professionals. The LEED Green Associate (GA) credential is the only entry level sustainability designation and shows employers and clients that you have certified knowledge in the green building industry.  A new LEED rating system (v4) was introduced last month and this training course is one of the few that has been updated to teach the current rating system. This course meets the exam’s eligibility requirements and the USGBC charges a $200 fee for the actual exam which can be taken at any time at your nearest Prometric center.
 
Cost: $300 ($200 for full time students)
To register for the class please visit: http://leadinggreen.ca/NYC

CUFSN/CU Health Workshops for Students with Families 

When: Weekly on Fridays, 1:00-2:00 pm (starting on Friday, October 10)
Where: Lewisohn Hall
What: Do you struggle to balance academic work with family responsibilities? How do your family responsibilities affect your participation in your department? Come to the Workshop for Students with Families hosted by the Columbia Family Support Network (CUFSN), in collaboration with Columbia Health, to discuss these issues in a supportive environment. We will cover a range of topics related to managing the stress and anxiety of your many roles. Attendees are also encouraged to suggest topics that they'd like to have covered, as these workshops are aimed at dealing with the issues you face as a student with a family. This workshop will be facilitated by Dr. Victoria Grosso and Dr. Andrew Colitz, clinical psychologists with Columbia Health, Counseling and Psychological Services. The weekly workshop begins Friday October 10th,1-2pm, Lewisohn Hall. It is open to all graduate and undergraduate students with families.
