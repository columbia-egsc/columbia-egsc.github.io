Graduate Student Class Day Speaker l  Nominate or Apply!


The Office of Graduate Student Affairs and the Dean's Office is currently accepting nominations for May 19th, 2014, SEAS Class Day Graduate Student Speaker.  SEAS staff, faculty and our graduate student body are invited to nominate a graduate student from the October 2013, February 2014 and/or candidates for the May 2014 graduate degree (MS, Professional, Eng.Sc.D., or PhD). 

Graduate students may also self-nominate, should they be interested and eligible for graduation. The nomination deadline is Friday, April 11th.  Guidelines and nomination forms are below.

Guidelines

  • All candidates, including those who are nominated, must submit a written speech and a Youtube video.
  • In the video, please present your speech as if you were speaking on Class Day. Show enthusiasm, good pace, annunciation and be well poised.
  • Your written submission should be grammar and spelling error free.
  • Students are encouraged to capture the essence of being a SEAS graduate student and share their thoughts about what lies ahead in the future.
  • Length of speech should be three minutes or less.
  • You will be evaluated on content, delivery and timing.
  • Voting will be conducted by the EGSC  and members from The Dean's Office and the Office of Graduate Student Affairs 
  • Students who are selected as final running candidates will be asked to present their speeches in front of the EGSC Executive Board and members from The Dean's Office and the Office of Graduate Student Affairs.


Nomination Forms

If you are Nominating a Student, fill out this form: http://bit.ly/1lICd1f  
(Students who are nominated will be emailed directly with further instructions.)

If you are Self-Nominating Yourself, please submit your nomination here: http://bit.ly/1ojvM6j



Graduate Student Life Award


On May 19th, 2014, many graduate students will be participating in SEAS Class Day. We are seeking nominations for our Graduate Student Life Award within this class year (October 2013, February 2014, and/or candidates for May 2014).

Two awards will be given: one to a Doctoral student and one to a Masters/Professional degree student.

Nominees must exemplify the following: 

  • Leadership, serving as a role model and rising engineer within her/his respective department, the Columbia Engineering community, as well as the Columbia University community as a whole.
  • Commitment to enhancing the quality of life for the Columbia Engineering and graduate student community.
  • Academic Excellence
  • Breadth, depth, and high quality of academic achievement and outstanding academic work beyond that which is required for the degree.
  • Creativity, innovation, and leadership in scholarly pursuits, including research, breadth of knowledge and teaching.
 

To nominate a student or apply for yourself, please submit this form
 
Students who are not nominated by another party and have applied on their own must have a faculty, staff or peer fill out a brief statement in the online form.

Deadline: Friday, April 4th

Own the room

The Dean’s Office and Office of Graduate Student Affairs invite you to a special session of Own the Room on:

Date:  Friday, April 4th
Time: 1:00 p.m. to 5:00 p.m.
Location: Davis Auditorium

Are you prepping for interviews? Presenting your research at conferences, or giving a talk to colleagues? Launching a start-up and need to perfect your elevator pitch? Then "Own the Room" is for you!

This is a great opportunity for both students and faculty to hone your public speaking and overall communication skills—this program will help you tell your story and “own the room.” You will improve your presentation skills, tap into your creative side, and rethink the power of storytelling in your professional life.

This session will be led by Own the Room CEO Bill Hoogterp, who has developed a methodology and technology that helps people quickly become better speakers, presenters, and communicators, all in a fun, collegial atmosphere. He’s trained all kinds of people, including students, faculty, business executives, athletes, celebrities, and many other professionals. (http://www.owntheroom.com/about/bios)

For more info, please watch: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hL063MerpQw
or visit http://www.owntheroom.com/

ADI D3.js Workshops


PART 1: Monday, 03/31, 09:00 PM — 10:00 PM at Studio@Butler

PART 2: Tuesday, 04/01, 09:00 PM — 10:00 PM at Studio@Butler

Got awesome data you want to display? Interested in, but never got around to learning how to make awesome visualizations for the web? Come to this 2-day workshop to get a taste of D3.js - a powerful JavaScript library used to bring data to life. The first night will be an introduction to D3.js: how to approach and use the library. It assumes very basic HTML / CSS knowledge, and zero prior knowledge about D3.js. The second night will be a continuation of the previous night's workshop: more advanced D3.js techniques will be discussed and covered.
