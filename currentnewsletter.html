James Webb Space Telescope: The first light machine


When: Wednesday, March 25, 5:30 pm
Where: 750 CEPSR
What: Scheduled to begin its 10 year mission after 2018, the James Webb Space Telescope (JWST) will search for the first luminous objects of the Universe to help answer fundamental questions about how the Universe came to look like it does today. At 6.5 meters in diameter, JWST will be the world’s largest space telescope. This talk reviews science objectives for JWST and how they drive the JWST architecture, e.g. aperture, wavelength range and operating temperature. Additionally, the talk provides an overview of the JWST primary mirror technology development and fabrication status.

CTV Fellows Program
Columbia Technology Ventures is now accepting applications for the nationally-recognized CTV Fellows Program (deadline is March 11, 2015).The CTV Fellows Program is open to all Columbia graduate students who are US citizens or permanent residents. We especially encourage applications from graduate students seeking degrees in scientific, engineering, clinical and business disciplines. The Program offers the chance for Columbia graduate students to gain experience with university technology transfer and technology commercialization. Fellows are paid $25 per hour and work on a remote basis up to 10 hours per week. The online application may be accessed at http://bit.ly/ctvfellows2015.

ABOUT THE CTV FELLOWS PROGRAM
Established in 2006, the Fellows Program at Columbia Technology Ventures (CTV) offers a unique opportunity for Columbia graduate students to gain in-depth experience with university technology transfer and technology commercialization. Fellows work closely with CTV on a wide range of projects, including assessment of the commercial viability of new inventions, identifying potential licensees or investors, developing marketing materials, and analysis of patent literature. Over the years, the 120+ graduating Fellows alumni have gone on to distinguished careers in STEM fields and elsewhere, working for companies as diverse as Intel, IBM, Merck, Novartis, Apple, The Boston Consulting Group, McKinsey, Osage Ventures, various law firms, and in tech transfer at universities such as UCSF, U Washington, Albert Einstein, and UCLA.

ABOUT COLUMBIA TECHNOLOGY VENTURES
Columbia Technology Ventures (CTV) manages Columbia University's inventions and intellectual property portfolio and serves as Columbia's gateway for companies, investors, and entrepreneurs seeking novel technology solutions and business opportunities. Columbia inventions have contributed to advances in a wide range of fields and markets, resulting in hundreds of products and services that touch the daily lives of people all over the world. This success is due to an exceptional base of world-class scientists working in diverse and dynamic areas of science and engineering.

Career Pathways for PhDs: Conversations with Alumni

Date: March 11, 2015 from 6:00 pm to 8:00 pm EDT
Location: Columbia Alumni Center, Schapiro Room
622 West 113th Street (Between Broadway and Riverside Drive)
Contact: For further information regarding this event, please contact Donald Goldstein by sending email to dg2662@columbia.edu or by calling 212-854-5609.


The Career Pathways for PhDs: Conversations with Alumni is an opportunity for PhD students to discuss careers outside of the academy with GSAS alumni in a small group setting.  The speakers will candidly share their experiences navigating the job search process and successfully landing new careers.  Students will have the opportunity to ask questions and learn about career options, job search skills, career transitions and networking skills.

Our speaker will be Anita Demkiv, a commodities expert in oil and energy.

Anita Demkiv was assigned to Ukraine when she volunteered for the Peace Corps from 1999 to 2001.  She taught at Odessa National University.  The Peace Corps volunteers not only taught classes but also engaged in an exchange of cultures with the people there.  She became charmed by the culture and gained a level of affection for the Ukrainian people.  She stayed in the country for another year and a half as a coordinator of the International Renaissance Foundation, a consultant for the World Bank in Kiev, and as a Peace Corps volunteer trainer.  Her experiences in Ukraine were the springboard for pursuing an M.A. in Russian, Eurasian, and Eastern European Studies.  She became fascinated with the post-Soviet legacy and its lasting influence. Anita holds a PhD in Global Affairs with research emphasis on petroleum exporting states and political instability, and is a commodities expert in oil and energy.

Eligibility: Open to all PhD students from the Graduate School of Arts and Sciences

Registration: Required

Attire: Casual
