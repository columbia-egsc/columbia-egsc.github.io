SEAS Research Showcase



Please join the Engineering Graduate Student Council for the SEAS Research Showcase. The showcase will feature presentations on topics relating to student and group research endeavors from 6 departments in the School of Engineering and Applied Science. These presentations are designed to give students the opportunity to learn about some of the exciting research being done at Columbia.

The talk will be held on Wednesday December 3rd, 2014 from 1:00 pm to 3:00 pm in Davis Auditorium. Refreshments to follow.

Presenters

  • Kacey Ronaldson, Biomedical Engineering, Topic: Electromechanical Conditioning of Functional Human iPSCell Derived Cardiac Microtissues for Predictive Toxicity Studies
  • Ni Ma, Industrial Engineering and Operations Research, Topic: Stabilizing Performance in a single-server queue with time-varying arrival rate
  • Wilkie Choi, APAM,:Nuclear Fusion on Locked mode control in Tokamaks.
  • Kevin Knehr, Chemical Engineering: Understanding the Formation of Lead Sulfate Films in Lead-acid Batteries
  • Josh Browne, EAEE
  • Lee Zhu, Electrical Engineering

CUFSN/CU Health Workshops for Students with Families 

When: Weekly on Fridays, 1:00-2:00 pm (starting on Friday, October 10)
Where: Lewisohn Hall
What: Do you struggle to balance academic work with family responsibilities? How do your family responsibilities affect your participation in your department? Come to the Workshop for Students with Families hosted by the Columbia Family Support Network (CUFSN), in collaboration with Columbia Health, to discuss these issues in a supportive environment. We will cover a range of topics related to managing the stress and anxiety of your many roles. Attendees are also encouraged to suggest topics that they'd like to have covered, as these workshops are aimed at dealing with the issues you face as a student with a family. This workshop will be facilitated by Dr. Victoria Grosso and Dr. Andrew Colitz, clinical psychologists with Columbia Health, Counseling and Psychological Services. The weekly workshop begins Friday October 10th,1-2pm, Lewisohn Hall. It is open to all graduate and undergraduate students with families. To find out more or to RSVP, please contact Francine (flk2107@columbia.edu).

Enhancing Your Relationship: A Workshop for Couples

This hands-on, sequential 4-session workshop will provide practical tools for couples who wish to strengthen their relationship. Topics include:
 - Understanding Relational Expectations and Beliefs
 - How to Nurture the Positives in the Relationship
 - How to Improve Problem Solving Ability and Communication Skills
