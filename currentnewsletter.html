 STEMulation: Connecting students with professionals to promote STEM careers




JPMC Civic Engagement seeks to connect high school girls interested in science, technology, engineering and math (STEM) with professional women working in STEM-fields at universities, labs, companies and organizations.  Relationships with role models and mentors have a significant impact on young people as they consider and pursue STEM-related fields of study and careers. And, most young women who pursue STEM majors and careers indicate that they became interested in those disciplines during high school and some as early as middle school. We believe that connecting girls with each other, as well as with potential role models and mentors (especially women), will help our students further develop, nurture and maintain interest in these fields.

 
Date:                     Monday, October 12th, 2015
Time:                     12:00 p.m. – 2:00 p.m. (Doors open at 11:45 a.m.)
Location:              383 Madison Avenue, Conference Center, New York

Role of the professionals:

Each of the professionals will be given 4 table assignments upon arrival (1 for lunch and 3 for networking) and will be asked to simply converse with the girls at each table about their lives, professions, passions and give basic advice where applicable.  Every 15 minutes, professionals will be prompted to move to their next table assignment until the networking session has ended.
Professionals will not be required to take on mentees but are being asked to share their professional contact information and be willing to take follow up messages from students.
Professionals will not be required to address the audience but they will likely be acknowledged by name & company during the opening or closing remarks.

Interested parties should send an email at Julian.S.Clayton@jpmchase.com with their full name, field, company, years of experience in the field, email and phone number.
