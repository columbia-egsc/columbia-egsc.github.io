Professional Development

WE Pitch

The Office of Graduate Student Affairs invites you to participate in our Spring Pitch Festival on Friday, April 17 and Saturday, April 18th.
 
About the Competition
Have an innovative idea, want to develop it, but don't know how? Come to Columbia University's WE Pitch and meet our mentors who can help bring your idea to fruition. Experience not required! Pitches at every stage of development encouraged -- We will be with you from conception to execution.
 
To apply or for more information about the pitch fest and events, please see: http://wepitch.weebly.com
 
Eligibility: Team sizes can range anywhere from 1 to 4 people, and must comprise of at least 50% women. No experience necessary.
 
How to get involved?
If you are interested in participating in WE Pitch, join the mixer on Thursday, February 26 from 6-7:30 PM at Bettolona. This will give you an opportunity to build your team and meet new people. Food and drinks will be provided.
 
To register, please visit: http://bit.ly/1E4tsaH
 
About WE Pitch:
WE Pitch provides a supportive community of innovative women, where you can collaborate and receive mentorship for your ideas. The event includes free workshops on developing pitches, fine-tuning ideas, and building your company, as well as an opportunity to present and receive feedback from a panel of angel investors. The goal of this event is to empower women in entrepreneurship and to provide an opportunity to develop and showcase your talent and ideas.
 
WE Pitch will include workshops, judging and prizes!


Data Science Hackathon 2015

The first ever, university led, Data Science Hackathon is being held in NYC from March 6-8. Join Columbia and Cornell Tech students for a weekend of camaraderie and coding.  We encourage students from a variety majors, degrees, and years in school to work together to create a holistic data science solution using datasets from RAND, Accenture, Uber, etc.
 
The weekend includes several speakers, (Hilary Mason, Claudia Perlich, Drew Conway, Jake Hofman, Gilad Lotan, and more) tech talks, a panel, and the opportunity to hone your data science skills. You are not dreaming! This is really happening.
 
Apply now:  http://datahackathon2015.splashthat.com
~100 students will be accepted to attend this event held at Cornell Tech at 111 8th Ave in Chelsea.

Job Search Skills Workshop And ISSO Presentation For International Students


Date:    February 18, 2015 from 10:00 am to 12:00 pm EST
Location:    The Center for Career Education Conference Room
East Campus--Lower Level
(Enter off of 116th Street, east of Amsterdam Avenue through the Wien Courtyard)
Contact:    For further information regarding this event, please contact Donald Goldstein by sending email to dg2662@columbia.edu or by calling 212-854-5609.


The first hour of the workshop will be presented by CCE and will focus on the special cultural and language issues and challenges that International Students face in the job search process.  We will discuss the cultural norms of getting jobs in the U.S. including topics such as networking, business etiquette and body language. We will go over the importance of improving your English language abilities in order to be more effective in elevator pitches, interviews and written communications.  We will delve into suggested methods of language improvement including resources.

During the second hour of the workshop, a staff member of the ISSO will provide information for International Students who are interested in staying in the U.S. to work after completion of their programs.  You will receive valuable information and have the chance to ask questions about the F-1 visa Optional Practical Training including time lines and application procedures.

Attire: Casual
Registration: Required
Resume Required? No
Eligibility: This event is open to all international graduate and undergraduate students from GSAS, SEAS, GS, SOA, and Columbia College.
