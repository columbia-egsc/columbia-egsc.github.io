Graduate Engineer's connect




Thursday, October 15th, 2015.

7:00 PM to 9:00 PM (EDT)


Soulaymane Kachani, Senior Vice Dean, Columbia Engineering, invites Engineering alumni who hold MS, PhD, or EngScD degrees and current graduate students to attend Graduate Engineers Connect. This signature event for graduate students and alumni will feature keynote remarks by alumnus Dr. Shahram Ebadollahi MS'99 and PhD'05, Vice President, Innovations & Chief Science Officer at IBM Watson Health Group. Graduate Engineers Connect seeks to strengthen the Columbia Engineering graduate community by bringing over 200 graduate-level degree alumni, students, and post-doctoral fellows together for an evening of networking and information sharing.

Hors d'oeuvres and beverages will be served.
Recommended dress is business attire.

Global Warming Challenges, Energy Solutions



6:30-7:30PM, Wednesday, October 14
7:30PM-8:00PM, Networking, We're hiring!
Davis Auditorium (CEPSR 412)

Global warming represents one of humanity’s greatest challenges. The ways in which we produce and consume energy are the largest contributors to this global issue. During this talk, Mr. Keiser will discuss the mechanics and drivers of global warming, as well as some of the most viable solutions.

Richard Keiser is the Founder and CEO of Level Solar, one of New York’s largest solar providers. Prior to founding Level Solar, Mr. Keiser was a Senior Analyst at Sanford C. Bernstein, where he led the Technology Strategy team. In 2005, he was awarded the Sanford C. Bernstein Royal Reach Award for outstanding contributions to equity research. Prior to working at Sanford Bernstein, Richard was a management consultant at Booz Allen & Hamilton. He is a graduate of MIT.

Speaker Bio:

Mr. Adrien Lajous
Former PEMEX CEO

Adrián Lajous is a non-resident Fellow at the Center on Global Energy Policy. He has been Chairman of the Oxford Institute for Energy Studies. He also serves on the Board of Trustees of El Colegio de México. In 2003-04 he was a Senior Fellow at the Kennedy School of Government of Harvard University. From 2001 to 2011 he was senior energy advisor to McKinsey & Company. In 1994 Lajous was appointed Director General of Pemex (CEO) and Chairman of the boards of the Pemex group of operating companies. He stepped down from this position in December 1999 after 29 years in public service.


H.E. Manuel Pinho
Former Economy Minister of Portugal
Columbia SIPA Professor

Former Minister of Economy and Innovation, Portugal (2005- 09), was the President of the EU Council of Energy Ministers (2007). Manuel Pinho is credited for the energy reform that transformed Portugal in a world leader in clean energies and is the author of Europe´s New Energy Era, the background paper of the EU Strategic Energy Technological Plan. He has been a Visiting Professor at SIPA in 201- 2014, where is currently an Adjunct Professor. He holds a BA from the Technical University of Lisbon and a doctoral degree from the University of Paris-X. Manuel Pinho has Honors by the governments of Brazil, France, Germany, Norway, Spain and Sweden.
