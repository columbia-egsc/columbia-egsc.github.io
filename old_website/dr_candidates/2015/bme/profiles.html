Please describe any relevant past experience

I have served my department since my first year as Career Chair, then Vice President, and President of the Graduate BME Council, with responsibilities ranging from event planning to running meetings and coordinating with faculty on interview weekend and orientation. I served on the executive committee of TEDxColumbiaEngineering as the Marketing and Promotions Chair alongside graduate students from across SEAS. I have regularly attended EGSC meetings for the past year and volunteered in the Interschool Committee, as well as filling the role of this committee's chair over the summer by leading the planning committee for FestiFall.

What are your goals/vision for your committees of choice? How do you think you can contribute to them?

As a member of the planning committee for FestiFall, I would like to see the Columbia Graduate Council's activities expand beyond this annual social to offer other opportunities for schools across campus to come together in planning and enjoying an event. Continuity in leadership is always a challenge in the face of the rapid turnover of masters students, so I would like to develop a better framework for planning interschool events that is easy for new members to pick up and run with. I have great organizational skills, as well as contacts in many schools around campus, which will aid in strengthening the transition between active CGC members and interschool relations in general. I have done a fair amount of event planning, but often within the student body, and have not gotten a chance to really acquaint myself with the alumni community and how they can function as a resource. I would like to add my creativity and organizational skills to planning new types of events where students can not only meet alumni, but benefit from the meeting in a way that does not feel forced. I would also welcome a change from the usual event planning and work in Academic Affairs. I have excellent communication skills, which I think would be helpful in facilitating the conversations that must happen across student and administrative populations to make meaningful policy change.

I have been coming to EGSC meetings regularly for the past year, I just haven't been able to come to enough consecutively to make an impression, as I was busy running own department's graduate student council.
